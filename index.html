The Titanic

"The Unsinkable Ship” - one particular flaw in its structure, unfortunately, led to its devastating sinking.The walls that separated the watertight bulkheads were very close to the waterline, which in this case, led to water pouring into the ship `s compartments once it hit the iceberg. The faulty structure, along with the small number of lifeboats, further caused the catastrophic impact, leaving behind 1,517 people to die at sea, many of whose bodies have not yet been found. The sinking of the R.M.S Titanic is truly a heartbreaking historical remnant that will never be forgotten.

The Origin of the Rockefeller Christmas Tree

Rockefeller Center is absolutely beautiful during the holidays, and thousands visit its annual Christmas tree lighting, making it an American tradition. But the christmas tree origin remains quite unknown to the public, and many would find its humble origins surprising. The Christmas tree tradition at Rockefeller Center was started by Italian American workers, who decorated a 20-foot tree in Rockefeller Center with paper garlands, strings of small cranberries, and small tin cans. After the original decoration, workers started to collect money for their annual Christmas tree, while their families made garlands to adorn it. Soon enough, it became a holiday spectacle, where passersby would start taking notice, and as years went by, NBC decided to broadcast the tree lighting, spreading the word about America’s Christmas tree.

The Blue Volcano

The Kawah Ijen- a volcano in Indonesia - is really an amazing sight with beautiful blue lava falling from its edges. "[The Kawah Ijen has] extremely high quantities of sulfuric gases, [which] emerge at high pressures and temperatures (sometimes in excess of 600°C) along with the lava. Exposed to the oxygen present in air and sparked by lava, the sulfur burns readily, and its flames are bright blue".

The Trade of Manhatten

350 years ago, the Dutch traded Manhattan - currently the most popular tourist destination in New York City - for a British colonial island, called Run Island, in Indonesia, as it housed the Nutmeg seed, a popular spice that had more value than gold. Years later, the Bandanese (the inhabitants of Run Island) regained control of their land, but it is not as prized as it once used to be.

The Dollar Bill History

The original one-dollar-bill displayed Salmon P. Chase, who was President Abraham Lincoln’s treasury secretary. The public was doubtful on the impact of paper money, and they weren’t sure if it would be a good substitute or addition to gold and silver. However, the government started to use paper money to finance the Civil War, and soon enough, paper money entirely replaced gold and silver as a mode of currency.
